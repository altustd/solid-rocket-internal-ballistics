5  Internal Ballistics Core

5.1 What is Internal Ballistics?

Internal ballistics is the study of the physical and chemical processes occurring inside a solid rocket motor from ignition until burnout. It bridges propellant chemistry and external performance (thrust, trajectory).

The primary goal is to predict and control the pressure-time curve (\(P\)-\(t\) curve), which directly determines thrust, burn time, and total impulse.

5.2 Fundamental Balance Equations

5.2.1 1. Mass Conservation (Mass Generation)

The rate at which gas is generated by the burning propellant must equal the rate at which gas is expelled through the nozzle (plus any accumulation in the chamber):

\[ \frac{dm}{dt} = \dot{m}_\text{gen} - \dot{m}_\text{out} \]

Where: - \(\dot{m}_\text{gen} = \rho_p \cdot A_b \cdot r\) (mass generation rate) - \(\rho_p\) = propellant density - \(A_b\) = burning surface area - \(r\) = linear burning rate

5.2.2 2. Energy and Pressure Relationship

Using the ideal gas law and isentropic assumptions, chamber pressure is related to the mass in the chamber:

\[ P_c = \frac{m R T_c}{V_c} \]

5.2.3 3. Nozzle Flow (Choked Flow)

For typical rocket motors operating with choked flow at the throat:

\[ \dot{m}_\text{out} = \frac{P_c A_t}{\sqrt{T_c}} \cdot \Gamma \sqrt{\frac{\gamma}{R}} \]

5.3 The Equilibrium Pressure Concept

In steady-state operation, mass generation equals mass expulsion:

\[ \rho_p A_b r = \frac{P_c A_t C_d}{\sqrt{T_c}} \]

This relationship leads to the famous Saint-Robert’s Law coupling with burning surface area:

\[ P_c = \left( \frac{\rho_p a A_b}{A_t C_d} \right)^{\frac{1}{1-n}} \]

Where: - \(a, n\) = burning rate coefficients (\(r = a P^n\)) - \(C_d\) = discharge coefficient
