7 Burning Rate, Kn Ratio, and Ballistic Performance

7.1 Learning Objectives

  • Understand the difference between strand burning rate and motor burning rate
  • Learn the Saint-Robert’s burning rate law (r = a P^n)
  • Define and calculate the Kn ratio (Kn = A_b / A_t)
  • Understand how Kn controls chamber pressure
  • Predict basic pressure-time and thrust-time curves

7.2 Burning Rate Fundamentals

The burning rate (r_b) is the speed at which the propellant surface regresses.

In strand burners (Crawford bomb), we measure burning rate under controlled pressure.

In actual motors, the burning rate is influenced by:

  • Chamber pressure (P_c)
  • Propellant initial temperature
  • Erosive burning (high gas flow velocity)
  • Dynamic burning (transient effects)

7.2.1 Saint-Robert’s Law (Vieille’s Law)

\[ r_b = a \cdot P_c^n \]

Where: - a = burning rate coefficient - n = pressure exponent (usually 0.2 – 0.7 for stable motors) - P_c = chamber pressure

7.3 The Kn Ratio

The Kn ratio is one of the most important parameters in solid rocket motor design:

\[ Kn = \frac{A_b}{A_t} \]

  • A_b = instantaneous burning surface area
  • A_t = nozzle throat area

Why it matters:

For steady-state burning, chamber pressure is approximately:

\[ P_c \approx \left( \frac{Kn \cdot a \cdot \rho_p \cdot c^*}{1} \right)^{\frac{1}{1-n}} \]

(where c* is characteristic velocity)

Higher Kn → Higher chamber pressure → Higher burning rate → Even higher pressure (positive feedback until equilibrium).

7.4 Pressure-Time Relationship

The shape of the pressure-time curve is largely determined by how A_b changes with time (i.e., grain geometry):

  • Neutral burning: A_b roughly constant → flat pressure curve
  • Progressive burning: A_b increases → rising pressure
  • Regressive burning: A_b decreases → falling pressure

7.6 Exercises

  1. Given a = 0.005 mm/s/MPa^n, n = 0.4, calculate burning rate at 5 MPa and at 10 MPa.

  2. If a motor has A_b = 500 cm² and A_t = 5 cm², what is Kn?

7.7 Key Takeaways

  • Burning rate is pressure-dependent (r = a P^n)
  • Kn ratio (A_b / A_t) is the primary design lever for controlling chamber pressure
  • Grain geometry controls how Kn evolves during the burn → controls pressure and thrust profile
  • Understanding Kn is essential before moving to full internal ballistics simulations
